# Composition Analysis

**Figure Placement:** The central figure—a man in a light-colored, open-collared shirt—occupies the right two-thirds of the frame in a medium close-up. His body is turned away from the camera while his head is rotated back over his left shoulder, creating a dynamic twisting pose. His face sits roughly along the right-third vertical line, following classical rule-of-thirds composition.

**Lighting:** The lighting is high-contrast and dramatic, characteristic of low-budget genre filmmaking of the era. A strong key light comes from the upper left, illuminating the left side of his face, forehead, and the curve of his cheekbone, while casting deep shadows into his right eye socket and the right side of his face. The shirt catches the light prominently, creating a luminous white shape that anchors the lower portion of the frame. Background elements are lit more flatly, creating separation between subject and setting.

**Set Elements/Props:** Behind the figure are several large rectangular panels or canvases leaning against a wall—possibly abstract paintings, room dividers, or set flats. Diagonal beams or struts (perhaps an easel, ladder, or scaffolding) cut across the upper left, introducing strong geometric lines. A horizontal ledge or counter is visible at the bottom left. The setting suggests an artist's studio, workshop, or similarly improvised interior space.

**Camera Angle/Framing:** The camera is positioned at roughly eye level or very slightly below, lending the figure a subtle sense of stature and presence. The tight framing crops the top of his head and cuts him off at mid-torso, intensifying intimacy and psychological focus. His backward glance toward the lens creates tension—as if he's been interrupted or is reacting to something off-screen behind him—a common suspense device. The diagonal background lines counterbalance his turned posture, adding visual unease to an otherwise contemplative moment.

This composition (and the film's date) strongly suggests this is a still of Duane Jones from George A. Romero's *Night of the Living Dead* (1968).
